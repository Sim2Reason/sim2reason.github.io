    ]
        \begin{scriptsize}
            
        A rocket is launched normal to the surface of the Earth, away from the Sun, along the line joining the Sun and the Earth. 
        \vspace{0.5em}
        
        \textbf{Parameters:}
        \begin{itemize}[nosep, leftmargin=1.5em]
            \item Mass of Sun = $300,000 \times$ Mass of Earth
            \item Distance = $25,000 \times$ Radius of Earth
            \item Escape Velocity (Earth) = $11.2\text{ km/s}$
        \end{itemize}
        \vspace{0.5em}
        The minimum initial velocity ($v_S$) in km/s required for the rocket to be able to leave the Sun-Earth system is closest to:\\
        \textbf{[A]} $22$ \quad \textbf{[B]} $42$ \quad \textbf{[C]} $62$ \quad \textbf{[D]} $72$
        \end{scriptsize}
    \end{tcolorbox}

    \vspace{-0.5em}

    \noindent
    \begin{minipage}[t]{0.49\linewidth}

        \begin{tcolorbox}[
            title={\scriptsize \textbf{BASE MODEL}},
            colframe=blue!60!white,

            coltitle=white,
            fontupper=\scriptsize\ttfamily\raggedright,

            sharp corners,
            left=2pt, right=2pt,
            width=\linewidth,
            nobeforeafter
        ]
            \textbf{\textcolor{blue!60!black}{Step 1:}} Calculate Escape Velocity from Sun\\
            Using the given ratios ($M_{\text{Sun}} = 3\times 10^5 M_E$, $D = 2.5\times 10^4 R_E$):\\\hfill\\
            $v_{\text{sun}} = \sqrt{2 G M_{\text{Sun}} / R}$\\
            $v_{\text{sun}} \approx 54.78\text{ km/s}$
            \colorbox{red!15}{\textcolor{red!80!black}{\textbf{ERROR}: }}
            \colorbox{red!15}{\textcolor{red!80!black}{Correct $\approx$ 39 km/s}}
            \vspace{0.3em}
            
            \vspace{1em}
            
            \textbf{\textcolor{blue!60!black}{Step 2:}} Calculate Total Velocity\\
            To escape both bodies, we sum the required velocities:\\\hfill\\
            $v_{\text{total}} = v_{\text{earth}} + v_{\text{sun}}$\\
            $v_{\text{total}} = 11.2 + 54.78\text{ km/s}$
            
            % ERROR HIGHLIGHT
            \vspace{0.3em}
            \colorbox{red!15}{\textcolor{red!80!black}{\textbf{ERROR}: }}
            \colorbox{red!15}{\textcolor{red!80!black}{Linear addition}}
            \vspace{0.3em}
            
            $v_{\text{total}} \approx 65.98\text{ km/s}$
            \vspace{1em}
            
            \textbf{Conclusion:}\\
            The calculated value ($66\text{ km/s}$) is closest to option [C].
            \vspace{1em}
            
            \textbf{Predicted Answer: [C] 62 km/s (Incorrect)}
        \end{tcolorbox}

            \textbf{\textcolor{green!40!black}{Step 1:}} Analyze the System\\
            We need to escape the combined potential of Earth and Sun.\\
            - Earth Escape Velocity: $11.2\text{ km/s}$\\
            - Sun Escape Velocity (at Earth's distance): $\approx 42\text{ km/s}$ (based on standard solar data)
            \vspace{1em}
            
            \textbf{\textcolor{green!40!black}{Step 2:}} Determine Effective Velocity\\
            Since the Sun's gravitational well is significantly deeper than Earth's, the escape velocity is dominated by the Sun.\\\\
            $v_{\text{total}} = \sqrt{v_{\text{sun}}^2 + v_{\text{earth}}^2}$
            \vspace{0.5em}

            \vspace{0.3em}
            \colorbox{green!15}{\textcolor{green!80!black}{\textbf{Correct}:}}
            \colorbox{green!15}{\textcolor{green!80!black}{Energy Addition}}

            Because $v_{\text{sun}} (42) \gg v_{\text{earth}} (11)$, the result is approximately $v_{\text{sun}}$.\\
            $v_{\text{total}} \approx 42\text{ km/s}$
            \vspace{1em}
            
            \textbf{Conclusion:}\\
            The minimum velocity is dictated by the Sun's gravity.
            \vspace{1em}
            
            \textbf{Predicted Answer: [B] 42 km/s (Correct)}
