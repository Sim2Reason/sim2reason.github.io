        A small mass ($m = 0.10$ kg) moving with initial speed $u = 5.00$ m/s collides elastically with a bar (Mass $M = 1.00$ kg, Length $L = 0.20$ m) that is pivoted at one end. The collision occurs at a distance $L/2$ from the pivot.
        \vspace{0.5em}
        
        \textbf{Parameters:}
        \begin{itemize}[nosep, leftmargin=1.5em]
            \item $m = 0.10$ kg; $u = 5.00$ m/s
            \item $M = 1.00$ kg; $L = 0.20$ m
            \item $I_{bar}$ (about pivot) $= ML^2 / 3$
        \end{itemize}
        \vspace{0.5em}
        The velocity of the small mass ($v$) after the collision is closest to:\\
        \textbf{[A]} $4.30\text{ m/s}$ \quad \textbf{[B]} $5.00\text{ m/s}$ \quad \textbf{[C]} $-2.50\text{ m/s}$ \quad \textbf{[D]} $2.15\text{ m/s}$
    \end{tcolorbox}

            title={\small \textbf{BASE MODEL}},

            \textbf{\textcolor{blue!60!black}{Step 1:}} Conservation of Angular Momentum\\
            $L_{\text{initial}} = m \cdot u \cdot (L/2)$\\
            $L_{\text{initial}} = 0.1 \times 5.0 \times 0.1 = 0.005$
            
            \vspace{0.3em}
            \colorbox{red!15}{\textcolor{red!80!black}{\textbf{ERROR}: Arithmetic (Should be 0.05)}}
            \vspace{0.3em}
            
            $L_{\text{final}} = I_{\text{pivot}} \omega + m v (L/2)$\\
            $0.005 = 0.00133 \omega + 0.005 v$
            \vspace{1em}
            
            \textbf{\textcolor{blue!60!black}{Step 2:}} Conservation of Kinetic Energy\\
            Since collision is elastic, conserve energy.\\
            $KE_{\text{initial}} = 0.5 m u^2 = 1.25\text{ J}$
            \vspace{0.5em}
            
            For the bar, we consider both rotation and translation:\\
            $KE_{\text{bar}} = 0.5 I_{\text{pivot}} \omega^2 + 0.5 M v_{\text{cm}}^2$
            
            \vspace{0.3em}
            \colorbox{red!15}{\textcolor{red!80!black}{\textbf{ERROR}: Double counting energy}}
            \vspace{0.3em}
            
            \colorbox{red!15}{\textcolor{red!80!black}{(Using $I_{\text{pivot}}$ already accounts for motion}}} \\
            \colorbox{red!15}{\textcolor{red!80!black}{of COM. Adding linear KE counts twice.)}}
            \vspace{1em}
            
            \textbf{\textcolor{blue!60!black}{Step 3:}} Solve System\\

            \textbf{\textcolor{green!40!black}{Step 1:}} Conservation of Angular Momentum\\
            Calculate initial angular momentum correctly:\\
            $L_{\text{initial}} = m \cdot u \cdot (L/2)$\\
            $L_{\text{initial}} = 0.10 \times 5.00 \times 0.10 = 0.05$
            \vspace{0.5em}
            
            $L_{\text{final}} = I_{\text{pivot}} \omega + m v (L/2)$\\
            $0.05 = (\frac{1}{3} \times 1.0 \times 0.2^2) \omega + 0.1 v (0.1)$\\
            $0.05 = 0.0133 \omega + 0.01 v$
            \vspace{1em}
            
            \textbf{\textcolor{green!40!black}{Step 2:}} Conservation of Kinetic Energy\\
            For a bar pivoting at the end, KE is purely rotational about pivot:\\
            $KE_{\text{bar}} = 0.5 I_{\text{pivot}} \omega^2$
            \vspace{0.5em}
            
            Equation:\\
            $1.25 = 0.05 v^2 + 0.00665 \omega^2$
            \vspace{1em}
            
            \textbf{\textcolor{green!40!black}{Step 3:}} Solve System\\
            From Momentum: $v = 5 - 1.33\omega$\\
            Substitute into Energy...\\
            Solving yields $\omega \approx 7.0\text{ rad/s}$.\\
            Substituting $\omega$ back to find $v$:\\
            $v = 5 - 1.33(7.0) \approx 4.30\text{ m/s}$
            \vspace{1em}
            
            \textbf{Conclusion:}\\
            Velocity decreases slightly to $4.30\text{ m/s}$.
